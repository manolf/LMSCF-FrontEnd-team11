How does it work?

 

Instead of going to the garbage the daily collected coffee grounds go to Hut & Stiel's production site where they are carefully turned in to mushroom substrate. The substrate goes in to bags and the bags go in to the incubational room. After 3 weeks of incubating they go to Nora's basement to fruit and to be harvested.

About us mushrooms

 

We have simple needs. We like the dark side. With water and good coffee we grow into the best versions of ourselves. We are full of protein, fiber and many vitamins. And all that in the absence of sun. We are pure magic.

 

More about our family

Time to start!

After months of hammering, disassembling, assembling, painting, cleaning, drilling, cleaning, fixing and then cleaning the basement started to appear like a 70 square meters mushroom farm. Nora started the production with 50 bags of incubated oyster mushroom (Pleurotus ostreatus) and 4 bags of Lion's Mane (Hericium erinaceus) that are from a Hungarian mushroom company.
